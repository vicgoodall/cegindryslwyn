About



In 2021, Rhys Griffith moved back home from Cardiff and opened Cegin Dryslwyn (“Dryslwyn Kitchen”). Nestled into the Carmarthenshire valley in the shadow of the 13th century Dryslwyn Castle, Griffith restored an old stone shed into the kitchen and dining room. Focused on an intimate experience, the dining room seats 20.







Having worked the restaurant scene over the past 15 years in Bristol and Cardiff, Griffith found himself homesick. After realising the produce on offer within west Wales, he realised that was all he needed to open a new place right here. With his sister Beth as his business partner, they opened the restaurant as a family business with a twist: they could only use ingredients from within west Wales.



Menus here can last as long as a week or be finished after a single night, depending on the best produce available. Cegin Dryslwyn has received rave reviews and become a local favourite for the fact it is always seasonal, always fresh, and never boring. Choose from the day’s specials or the five course tasting menu. The dramatic scenery makes for a fine view, but inside is warm, informal and friendly. We look forward to your visit. Book today.

FAQs

How often does the menu change?

Depending on produce, it can be as frequently as daily or we may stretch out to one week. To be honest - don't book based on what you've seen on Instagram, as it may well be we've moved on to something new!


Do you cater for vegetarians/ vegans?

Yes, we always have a vegan option (but we don't cover in-betweens such as pescatarianism).


I have a dietary requirement. Can I still book?

Due to the nature of our business, as much as we would like to manage all dietary requirements, we cannot make any guarantees. We manage them on an individual basis, so please do feel free to get in touch with details about your requirements, and we will try our best to accommodate you.


Do you have a dress code?

No, there's no dress code.
